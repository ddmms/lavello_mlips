## 2024-03-25 - Pre-compiling Regex in performance-critical loops
**Learning:** Initializing `re` matches inside loops without pre-compiling adds significant overhead. Profiling regex performance specifically in `parse_charge_mult` showed that dynamic matching creates a ~1.5x-2x performance bottleneck over 100k invocations compared to `re.compile()` at the module level. Bare `except:` clauses can mask system-level interrupts (like `KeyboardInterrupt`) and should be replaced with specific exceptions (e.g., `except ValueError:`).
**Action:** Always extract regex expressions into pre-compiled module-level constants (e.g., `RE_CHARGE`, `RE_XYZ`) instead of defining them inline, especially in frequently called parsing loops. Explicitly catch anticipated exceptions rather than using bare `except:`.
